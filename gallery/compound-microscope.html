Compound Microscope

Contributor: Yi-Ting Tu

A compound microscope consists of two lenses, the objective, and the eyepiece. The object (green) is very near the axis, representing a small object. The object is then magnified by the two lenses so that a large inverted virtual image (the intersection of the final orange extensions) is produced.
